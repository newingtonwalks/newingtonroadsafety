Over 100 families on and around Newington Road have signed a petition asking Fairfax County to build a sidewalk

Safety is a top priority among residents as the road has no safe pedestrian infrastructure for children, seniors, dog walkers, runners, and anyone traveling without a vehicle

  • Get Started
    Why else do residents want a sidewalk on Newington Road?
  • To access nearby parks
  • To support local businesses
  • To walk to their local library
  • To get groceries from the local grocery store
  • To reach nearby public transit options
  • To add a missing link to connect to other sidewalks
  • To walk their dog, go jogging, and exercise

    • Fairfax County has described the road as "extremely dangerous"

    A recent report by County staff outlined the many ways Newington Road is an accident waiting to happen noting it has "no pedestrian facilities" and it "likely does not meet current safety, visibility, and design standards" making it an "extremely dangerous situation for bicyclists and pedestrians"

    Adding a sidewalk to improve safety aligns with all of Fairfax County's strategic goals

    The County Board of Supervisors in 2022 endorsed the Safe Streets for All Program to "address systemic transportation safety issues for people walking, biking and using other forms of active transportation."

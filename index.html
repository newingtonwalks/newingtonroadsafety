    Fairfax County has described the road as "extremely dangerous"

      Newington Road--a high-traffic road used by 4,600 cars and over 400 trucks on a typical weekday--was described by Fairfax County Staff in a report that noted:
  • "no pedestrian facilities"
  • "does not meet current safety, visibility, and design standards"
  • "extremely dangerous situation for bicyclists and pedestrians"

    • Voice your support!

    Adding a sidewalk to improve safety aligns with all of Fairfax County's strategic goals

    The County Board of Supervisors in 2022 endorsed the Safe Streets for All Program to "address systemic transportation safety issues for people walking, biking and using other forms of active transportation."

    • Support safety!

    The Newington Road community wants to safely walk to local parks

    Residents cannot safely go to their local park, Levelle Dupell Park, because there is no sidewalk. Fairfax County Staff noted it is nearly impossible to reach the park without a car due to the lack of a sidewalk

    • Support access to parks!

    The Newington Road community wants to safely walk to support local businesses

    Several restaurants, delis, cafes, and a brewery are less than mile away but residents cannot safely walk to these businesses without a sidewalk.

    The Newington Road community wants to safely access community services

    Levelle Dupell Park, Kingstowne Center for Active Adults, Kingstowne Library, Wegmans Supermarket, CVS Pharmacy, NOVA Day Care, US Post Office, and more are located a short walk away but residents cannot reach them because there is no sidewalk.
